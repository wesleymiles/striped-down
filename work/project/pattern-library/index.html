Background

  • The agency started with templated designs a decade ago.
  • Clients wanted more customization so they pivoted to building each one from scratch.
  • Which brought customization, which the clients loved.
  • However, this lead to 3 problems…
  1. More work for both dev and design.
  2. Lack of standardization.
  3. Poor mobile ux. Designers delivered desktop mockups only, so devs were responsible for all other breakdowns. Because many design decisions were made by non-designers, the mobile UX wasn’t optimal.

Process

  1. I strongly championed and pitched the idea of creating a design system after embracing Brad Frost’s Atomic Design.
  2. Initial energy uncovered a new need: that we all speak the same language. For example, we banned the term banner.
  3. I joined a team to spend 2 months creating a 23 page design glossary naming and defining of every site piece. We audited the hundreds of instances of non-defined components, and standardized them.

The company was ready to move forward with the pattern building part months later.

Process Cont.

  1. Defined goals: improve mobile design & improve efficiency
  2. Decided on which patterns to bring forward from every existing site, based on:
    • Best practices for User Experience
    • Best practices for Lead Generation
    • The performance of our industry-specific sites
  1. Responsive wireframes: the design team and I took the chosen patterns and converted them to wireframes. Some were already optimized for conversion, where others needed adjustments.
  2. Considered flexibility: This is the big question with design systems… where can we bend the rules? We landed on if the structure changes, it's a new pattern.
  3. Repeated: 3 versions for the 35 main site sections. This meant designers had 3 options for every content block.

Solution

A design system of repeatable patterns and reusable code.


Components like buttons, animations, and menus are customized for each project, but the macro layouts are accounted for in the library.

It’s regularly maintained. New ones requested, old ones retired.

Why this solution works:

  • Shares knowledge between teams better
  • Takes design-weight off dev
  • Sites are more conversion optimized
  • CRO insight built into process

What would I change?

This was a necessary stage in systematizing technology for this agency. It's young compared to more mature design systems, but it showed the efficiencies that they can bring.

  • We have room to make the patterns more general to offer more reuse. Now they are 100% tied to content.
  • Another big time-saver would be making the patterns live with a WYSIWYG, like squarespace or hupspot.

Metrics/Impact

  • After a year, they’re achieving conversion rate standards.
  • Custom design for clients: No sacrifice of quality, still a non-templated solution.
  • Data-Driven User Interface based on best practices and internal performance.
  • Decreased dev’s and design’s build-time by a full day.
