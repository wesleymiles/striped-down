An obligatory peek behind the curtain

The process of a site design within our team of 3.

  1. Discovery

    2. Designers are provided a questionnaire from the client including a vision, a brand, assets, and specific goals. Then we hold a video call where we fill in any gaps needed to nail the design.

    My responsibility is to design a site that meets these goals:

    1. Align branding to feel 100% custom
    2. Optimize for lead conversion
    3. Wow the client by acheiving their goals
    4. Adhere to the design system and follow SEO guidelines

  2. Research

    3. Here, I learn about their competitors, their audience, and consider what general style we‚Äôll be using. I still use Dribbble during this stage, especially if there‚Äôs a challenging color to work with. In addition, I scour my 10 years of evernote snippets for relevant solutions.

  3. Style guide

    4. At this stage ,the messy double-diamond research comes to a point to make decisions and put findings in order. It‚Äôs not a linear process though, (nothing really is). I throw ideas into the style guide then take them for a spin on the artboard to see if they stick. The creation of the design happens along-side the actual design, together.

    The logo is central to a brand. I use it and any other marketing materials provided to flesh out the style guide with textures, shapes, and motifs.

  4. Design the thing

    5. This is the intuitive, gut-stage and the hardest to write about.
